Talent Mobility Kata #1

Design your Heroes badge

Every badge tells a story! Over the next 20 minutes, work individually and use AI to design a Heroes Badge for your assigned theme — ready for real-world use. Your design may become a real badge, so make it meaningful, scalable, reusable, and uniquely yours!

!
Ground rules — read before you start

Animals, objects, or characters Prefer animals over humans. Symbolic objects also work well. If using people, include two or more characters from diverse groups.

Use EPAM DIAL Pick any of the models suitable for image generation (our recommendation is OpenAI GPT Image 1.5).

Be inclusive and respectful No offensive imagery, no sarcastic or negative meanings, nothing touching on race or gender.

Be precise, not vague Use as many descriptive details as possible. Vague prompts produce inconsistent results.

No text on the badge Do not add any text on the image itself, because AI often distorts text.

Keep it simple Avoid too many small objects in one image. One clear subject reads much better at small sizes.

3
Finalize badge details

Once you have your image, decide on these three things (you can use AI to generate or refine your ideas, but don't ask AI to add the text to the image — keep all text separate):

  • Badge name (2–5 words)
  • Badge description (1–2 short sentences)
  • Text on badge (1–3 words)

Share your result

Place the image and all text on the Miro board in the designated section.

5
Vote for your favorite design

Once everyone has added their results to Miro, review the designs, choose your favorite, and leave an emoji reaction on it.

The most popular designs will become the prototypes for the real badges!
